## Step 3: The Purchase Process

The Spanish property buying process follows a clear sequence:

### 1. Property Search & Selection
- Work with reputable estate agents
- Visit properties in person when possible
- Research the area, including future development plans

### 2. Reservation Agreement (*Contrato de Reserva*)
- Typically ‚Ç¨3,000-10,000 deposit
- Takes property off the market for 2-4 weeks
- Usually deducted from final purchase price
- **Important:** Ensure this is refundable if due diligence reveals problems

### 3. Due Diligence Period
Your lawyer will verify:
- **Nota Simple**: Official property registry extract
- **Cadastral reference**: Property boundaries and tax value
- **Outstanding debts**: Community fees, mortgages, taxes
- **Building licenses**: Legal construction status
- **Occupancy certificate** (*C√©dula de Habitabilidad*)

### 4. Private Purchase Contract (*Contrato de Arras*)
- Typically 10% of purchase price paid
- Legally binding agreement
- Sets completion date (usually 4-8 weeks)
- Penalties for withdrawal:
  - Buyer withdraws: Loses deposit
  - Seller withdraws: Must return double the deposit

### 5. Notary Signing (*Escritura P√∫blica*)

## Step 6: Financing Your Purchase

### Mortgages for Foreign Buyers

Spanish banks do offer mortgages to non-residents, but with stricter conditions:

**Typical Requirements:**
- **Down payment:** 30-40% minimum (vs. 20% for residents)
- **Income documentation:** Last 2-3 years of tax returns
- **Debt-to-income ratio:** Maximum 30-35% of net income
- **NIE number:** Required before application
- **Spanish bank account:** For mortgage payments

### Recommended Banks for Foreign Buyers

- BBVA
- CaixaBank
- Sabadell

> **üí∞ Tip:** Get a mortgage pre-approval before seriously searching. This helps with negotiations and shows sellers you're a serious buyer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can foreigners buy property in Spain?

Yes, there are no restrictions on foreigners buying property in Spain. You'll need an NIE number, but nationality doesn't affect your right to purchase.

### How long does the buying process take?

Typically **2-3 months** from accepted offer to completion. This can be shorter for cash purchases or longer if there are complications with documentation.

### Do I need to be in Spain to buy?

No. You can grant power of attorney (*poder notarial*) to your lawyer to act on your behalf, though most buyers prefer to attend the final signing.

### What's the difference between resale and new build purchases?

- **Resale:** Transfer Tax (ITP) applies, typically 6-10%
- **New build:** VAT (IVA) at 10% plus Stamp Duty (AJD) at 1-1.5%

### Should I buy through a company?

For most residential purchases, buying personally is simpler and cheaper. Company purchases may make sense for high-value properties or specific tax situations‚Äîconsult a tax advisor.
